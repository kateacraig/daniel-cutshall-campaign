Daniel's Values

Any decision I make as a county commissioner would ultimately center around one question: What is best for the people?

As a county commissioner, I would seek input from constituents, research the relevant topics, and ultimately seek to make data-based decisions that benefit the public. Three guiding principles which would influence my decision-making process are:

  1. Affordability. As our area experiences growth, we need to ensure that people are not getting priced out. Everyone deserves to live a happy and comfortable life.
  2. Environmental Stewardship. We only have one Earth. We need to work together to treat it with respect and care so that future generations can enjoy the natural beauty of our home.
  3. Equity. All people should be treated with respect and dignity. Opportunities should be available to everyone, and special care should be taken to make sure that no group is discriminated against.

Projects I am extremely proud to have been involved with include:

  • Go Baby Go Appalachia! Every year Milligan Engineering modifies multiple kids' ride on jeeps so that differently abled kids are able to play in the same way that their peers do.
    Read More
  • Piedras Negras clean water. One of our senior projects in 2024 involved the design and implementation of an off-grid, solar powered water purification system. The system was designed for and implemented in a refugee camp in Piedras Negras, Mexico that is run by CrossRoads Missions.
    Read More
  • Hurricane Helene relief. Milligan engineering was able to design and implement a grid compatible solar energy system for a family of four and their community in the mountains of Burnsville, NC after hurricane Helene wrought destruction in the area.
    Read More

Volunteer projects with my church, St. John's Episcopal Church:

  • Melting Pot Meal Preparation. Every month I help prepare lunches for our unhoused community members after church. I have been participating in the melting pot program since I was a young teenager attending Munsey Memorial United Methodist Church. I credit my parents with teaching me how to generously give back to the community, particularly those who are less fortunate.
  • Refugee Resettlement. I had the opportunity to co-lead a team of parishioners who worked with Episcopal Migration Ministries (EMM) to relocate Afghan refugees to our area who had helped with U.S. military operations in their country.

I will continue to invest in our community as a County Commissioner. Together, we will continue to move Washington County forward.
